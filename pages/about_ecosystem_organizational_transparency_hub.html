El Inicio

Hack The Future nació en 2024 de una observación simple: los jóvenes de Manrique tienen un potencial increíble pero carecen de acceso a oportunidades en el mundo digital. Nuestro fundador, Carlos Rodríguez, vio esto de primera mano mientras trabajaba en el barrio, donde los jóvenes tenían talento pero no las herramientas para acceder a la economía global.

Determined to create a different approach, Sarah partnered with local community leaders to develop programs that strengthened existing structures rather than replacing them. This community-centered philosophy became the foundation of Impact Connect's operational model.

"Real change happens when communities have the tools and support to drive their own transformation. Our role is to amplify their efforts, not direct them."
- Sarah Chen, Founder & CEO

Our Operational Philosophy

Community-centered approach that prioritizes dignity, agency, and sustainable change

Community Leadership

We believe lasting change comes from within communities. Our role is to support and amplify local leadership, not replace it.

  • Local leaders design programs
  • Community members set priorities
  • Cultural context guides implementation

Strategic Partnerships

We work exclusively with established local organizations that have deep community roots and proven track records.

  • Rigorous partner vetting process
  • Long-term relationship building
  • Capacity building support

Dignity & Agency

Frequently Asked Questions

Addressing common concerns about our operations and impact

How do you ensure program effectiveness?

We use a rigorous monitoring and evaluation framework that includes baseline assessments, regular progress tracking, and independent impact evaluations. All programs undergo quarterly reviews with community partners, and we adjust strategies based on real-world results. Our commitment to transparency means all evaluation reports are publicly available.

What percentage of donations go directly to programs?

87% of all donations go directly to program implementation. This is significantly higher than the sector average of 75%. Our low overhead is achieved through efficient operations, technology automation, and strategic partnerships that reduce administrative costs while maintaining program quality.

How do you measure long-term sustainability?

We track programs for five years post-implementation to measure sustainability. Our key indicators include local leadership development, community ownership levels, and continued program operation without external support. Currently, 78% of our programs continue operating successfully three years after initial funding ends.

How do you ensure cultural competency?

All programs are designed and led by local community members who understand cultural context. Our international staff undergo extensive cultural competency training, and we maintain long-term partnerships with local organizations rather than implementing programs directly. Community feedback is integrated into all program decisions.

What is your approach to beneficiary dignity?

We believe in participatory program design where beneficiaries are partners, not recipients. All storytelling requires informed consent, individuals maintain control over their narratives, and programs are designed to strengthen rather than replace existing community structures. We never use imagery or language that diminishes human dignity.
